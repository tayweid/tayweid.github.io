Part D | When markets aren't the right tool

// when do we need strategic thinking? //

Part D pushes beyond market failures to explore situations where markets aren't even the right framework. Here we encounter public goods and common resourcesâ€”goods that are entirely externalities with no private component. These situations demand a completely different approach: strategic thinking and game theory. You'll discover why cooperation is sometimes impossible even when everyone would benefit, and how understanding these dynamics reveals the hidden logic behind everything from environmental policy to international relations.

Block D1 ~ Non-excludable goods

Public goods and common resources create market failures because it's impossible to exclude people from using them, leading to free-rider problems and the tragedy of the commons.
