Part 4 | General Linear Model

// build simple models of relationships //

Part 4 introduces you to the workhorse of empirical economics: regression analysis. You'll move from simply observing relationships to modeling them mathematically, learning to quantify how much one variable affects another while controlling for other factors. These aren't just statistical techniques—they're the foundation for policy analysis, business forecasting, and academic research. Whether you're predicting housing prices, measuring wage gaps, or evaluating program effectiveness, linear models give you the precision to turn economic intuition into quantified insights.
