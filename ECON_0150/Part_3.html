Part 3 | Building Models

"All models are wrong. Some are useful." - George Box

Part 3 is about building useful models. We start by building intuition for the distribution of the sample mean with the Central Limit Theorem. Then we introduce the idea of a regression model, which is a way to predict the value of one variable based on the value of another. We discuss how to understand when relationships are causal and how to estimate the effect of one variable on another. Then we close by discussing how to visualize, test, and interpret the results of a regression model.
